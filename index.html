Section 02
Types of Chargers

Understanding which charger is right for your apartment is critical for safety, cost, and long-term infrastructure planning.

🏠

AC Home Charger

3.3–7.4 kW · Type-2 / Wallbox

The safest and most practical option for residential basements. Overnight charging is load-friendly, easy to meter, and suitable for most EV models.

✅ Recommended for Apartments
⚡

AC Fast Charger

7.4–22 kW · IEC 62196 Type-2

Faster turnaround but demands higher load. Suitable only where the building has adequate spare capacity. Always conduct a load study first.

⚠️ Limited Use — Load Study First
🏭

DC Fast Charger

30–350 kW · CCS-2 / CHAdeMO

Designed for highways and commercial stations. Requires massive load upgrades and special permits. Not appropriate for residential use.

❌ Not for Residential Basements

Section 03
AOA Challenges

10 real, practical obstacles every Apartment Owners Association must understand and proactively plan for before enabling EV charging.

⚡
CHALLENGE — 01

Limited Electrical Load

Most apartment buildings lack sufficient spare power capacity to support even a handful of EV chargers running simultaneously.

💰
CHALLENGE — 02

High Infrastructure Cost

Wiring, sub-meters, distribution panels, cable trays, and safety devices involve significant upfront investment that must be fairly divided.

🔥
CHALLENGE — 03

Fire Safety Concerns

RWAs fear battery-related fires and often lack proper detection systems, CO₂ extinguishers, and emergency electrical cut-offs in parking areas.

📜
CHALLENGE — 04

Regulatory Confusion

Unclear and overlapping norms from Government, DISCOM, BIS, and IS/IEC create hesitation and costly delays in decision-making.

🧾
CHALLENGE — 05

Billing Issues

Accurately tracking each resident's electricity consumption per charger and collecting payments fairly requires smart metering infrastructure.

🅿️
CHALLENGE — 06

Parking Constraints

Not all parking slots are physically accessible for cable routing — distance from the panel, pillar positions, and floor layout complicate installation.

🤝
CHALLENGE — 07

Vendor Inconsistency

Many vendors offer incomplete, uncertified, or misleading solutions. AOAs lack technical expertise to evaluate charger quality and compliance.

⚠️
CHALLENGE — 08

Risk of Overload

Simultaneous EV charging by multiple residents without load management can trip the building's main supply, affecting all residents.

🏘️
CHALLENGE — 09

Resident Conflicts

Different expectations on charger speed, cost-sharing, priority access, and safety responsibilities cause interpersonal friction in communities.

🗓️
CHALLENGE — 10

No Long-Term Planning

Uncertainty about future EV adoption rates makes load forecasting, infrastructure sizing, and phased upgrade planning extremely difficult.

Section 04
Fire Risk Causes

Understanding root causes of EV charger fires is the first step toward prevention. Each of these is avoidable with proper planning and certified installation.

🏷️
RISK — 01

Poor Quality Chargers

Low-grade EVSE without IS/IEC certification overheats under sustained load, lacks protection circuits, and may fail catastrophically.

🔧
RISK — 02

Improper Wiring

Loose terminals, undersized conductors, or poor crimping create high-resistance hotspots that build heat silently over time.

🔌
RISK — 03

Circuit Overload

Multiple chargers sharing a single circuit exceed its rated ampacity, causing cable insulation failure and breaker damage.

🛡️
RISK — 04

No Protection Devices

Without RCCB/RCBO (Type-A or Type-B), DC leakage currents and ground faults go undetected until serious damage occurs.

🌍
RISK — 05

Poor Earthing

High earth resistance allows fault currents to persist, generating heat in metallic enclosures and creating shock hazards.

🔋
RISK — 06

Extension Cords

Makeshift cables are not rated for continuous EV charging currents and overheat well before any protection device trips.

💨
RISK — 07

Poor Ventilation

Chargers in enclosed basement corners accumulate heat. Without airflow, thermal runaway becomes likely over months of use.

🔩
RISK — 08

Lack of Maintenance

Dust ingress, moisture, loose screws, and aging capacitors progressively degrade charger safety margins until failure occurs.

Section 06
Implementation Framework

A clear 8-step process for AOAs to enable EV charging systematically, safely, and fairly — from the first resident request through to ongoing maintenance.

📝
Step 01

Resident Request

Resident submits a formal written application to the AOA/RWA specifying EV make, model, preferred charger type, and designated parking slot number.

📄 Written application required
🔍
Step 02

Feasibility Check

AOA evaluates physical feasibility — cable routing path, parking slot access, distance from the electrical distribution panel, and structural constraints.

🏗️ Site evaluation
⚡
Step 03

Load Assessment

A licensed electrical engineer conducts a formal load study to determine whether the building's sanctioned load can accommodate additional EV charging demand safely.

👷 Licensed engineer required
✅
Step 04

Formal Approval

AOA grants written approval with clear conditions — only BIS-certified charger, only licensed installer, IS/IEC safety compliance mandatory, and sub-meter installation required.

📋 Written conditions issued
🔧
Step 05

Installation

Certified electrician installs the charger with MCB, RCCB Type-B, dedicated earthing, FRLS cabling, and conduit routing as per IS 732 and IEC 61851 standards.

🛡️ IS 732 + IEC 61851
🧪
Step 06

Testing & Inspection

Mandatory post-installation testing: earth continuity, insulation resistance (IR test), polarity check, RCCB trip-time verification, and visual inspection of all terminations.

🔬 IR + Earth continuity tests
🧾
Step 07

Billing Setup

Sub-meter or smart meter commissioned. Billing policy formalised: unit rate agreed, monthly billing cycle set, and payment mode decided between resident and AOA.

📊 Smart meter installed
🔄
Step 08

Ongoing Maintenance

Quarterly visual inspections scheduled. Residents briefed on usage rules, emergency protocols, and who to contact in case of any charger fault or unusual behaviour.

📅 Quarterly inspections
Section 07
Conclusion

Building an EV-ready community — with safety, governance, and sustainability as the foundation.

📐

Planning

Load studies, feasibility checks, and long-term capacity planning before any installation begins.

🛡️

Safety

Certified chargers, licensed electricians, proper earthing, protection devices, and fire safety systems.

🏛️

Governance

Formal RWA policies, written approvals, fair billing, and regular compliance audits for all residents.

Our communities are moving toward a cleaner and more convenient future by enabling safe, well-planned EV charging in basement parking areas. With proper wiring, certified chargers, and strong safety measures, EV charging becomes as routine and secure as any other household electrical use.


This initiative not only supports residents who already own electric vehicles, but also prepares our apartment complexes for the inevitable shift toward sustainable mobility. By following clear guidelines and cooperating with the RWA governance processes, every AOA can build an EV-ready community that is modern, responsible, and future-proof.


EV charging must be implemented with Planning, Safety, and Governance. These three pillars ensure that what begins as a convenience for a few becomes a safe, scalable, and fair infrastructure for the entire community.

⚠️ Disclaimer: This advisory is published by the Bangalore Apartments Federation (BAF) for general guidance purposes only. It does not constitute or replace a professional electrical safety assessment. AOAs and RWAs must ensure compliance with all applicable Central and State regulations, DISCOM norms, BIS standards, and IS/IEC safety codes before authorising or undertaking any EV charger installation.
